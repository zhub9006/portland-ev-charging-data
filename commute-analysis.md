# Sustainable Commute Analysis: Portland → Beaverton

## Route Summary

- **Origin:** Downtown Portland, OR (45.5159, -122.6822)
- **Destination:** Beaverton, OR (45.4872, -122.8038)
- **Distance:** ~12.8 km / 8 miles
- **Primary Route:** SW Broadway → Sunset Highway (US-26) → SW Beaverton-Hillsdale Highway → SW Farmington Road → SW Hall Boulevard

- **Distance:** 12.8 km / 8 mi
- **Duration:** ~2.5 hours
- **Route:** Same corridor, pedestrian paths along SW Broadway
- **Verdict:** ⚠️ Possible but impractical for daily commute

### 🚗 Car (Current Baseline)
- **Distance:** 12.8 km / 8 mi
- **Duration:** ~15 minutes
- **Route:** SW Broadway → Sunset Highway → SW Beaverton-Hillsdale Hwy → SW Farmington Road

### Corridor Gaps

⚠️ **Critical Gap:** No DC fast chargers identified along the Sunset Highway (US-26) corridor between downtown Portland and Beaverton.

- Most charging stations are clustered at either end of the route
- The Sunset Highway / Beaverton-Hillsdale corridor lacks mid-route charging
- This is a significant infrastructure gap for EV commuters

## Sustainable Commute Recommendations

### For EV Owners
1. **Charge at workplace** in Beaverton — eliminates en-route charging needs
2. **Use the Tesla Supercharger** at 4439 SW Beaverton-Hillsdale Hwy (325 kW) as destination charging
3. **Consider EV + bike combo** — EV for the main commute, bike for local errands
4. **Advocate for corridor charging** — the Sunset Highway gap needs DC fast chargers

### For Cyclists
1. **Bike commute at ~50 min is practical** — well within the range for a healthy daily commute
2. **No charging needed for cycling** — zero emissions, zero cost
3. **EV charging nearby** means EV owners can still access cycling as a complement

### For Planners
1. **Sunset Highway (US-26) corridor** needs DC fast charging infrastructure
2. **Bus/van electrification** could be supported by the heavy-duty chargers at Electric Island
3. **Bike lane improvements** along SW Broadway would enhance cycling safety
